2. Inspiration

I have been studying Korean for seven years. After a while, I had found a number of resources I liked. However, at a point the number of separate resources I was using became burdensome.

Initially, I only used a basic online dictionary. Later, I grew interested in Hanja characters and wanted to study them. Unfortunately, the online dictionary's Hanja dictionary is less than ideal, as its example words are not listed in order of usage or relevance.

To get a resource with a better order, I instead had a separate app installed. However, this app did not have a desktop or web version, so now I had different resource accessibility on different devices.

I later began using Anki, a flashcard program, to systematically reencounter the same words in the same context repeatedly. Making Anki flashcards was tedious, as I had to copy and paste information both the online dictionary and the Hanja app for almost every card. The flashcard's information also only existed in Anki, so if I ever looked at the online dictionary entry again for the word, none of my work would be there.

I was using the online dictionary and the Hanja app because I liked the default wealth of information they provided. But with Anki, I liked the ability to make my own cards, with sentences and contexts I personally found and experienced.

NEED TO IMPROVE THIS PART Later, I got a paper French dictionary. In the age of technology, a paper dictionary felt impractical. But when I looked inside I realized it was exactly what I was looking for. The previous owner left examples of a word they had seen for themselves in the margins of each line; the original page was just a default setting. They could add whatever they wanted.

Recreating this experience is the major premise of this Korean dictionary. Rather than copy and pasting to Anki, you can add notes, pictures, and example sentences directly into the entry for any word. You can mark words as 'known' and the dictionary will specifically prioritize those words when ordering Hanja words. And most importantly, there are no separate flashcards. Here, the entire dictionary is now the set of flashcards, and choosing to study any word just involves adding the dictionary entry into your study list.

This is all also user specific, so it is like every user has their own "copy" they can personally edit.

The dictionary also includes standard dictionary features like being able to click on any word to search for its meaning. For a complete list of features, view the repository(linkto) for the dictionary.
