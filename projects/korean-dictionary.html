1. Summary

A dictionary for intermediate+ Korean learners that combines my favorite features from several standard resources. Users can add notes, pictures, and example sentences directly onto the page for any word. This allows them to create personally tailored entries for the words they encounter. Also includes review systems and games to further facilitate all-in-one-place learning.

2. Inspiration and Concept

I have been studying Korean since 2018. A few years in, I had found a number of resources I liked. However, at a point the number of separate resources I was using became burdensome.

Initially, I only used a basic online dictionary (Naver Dictionary). Later, I grew interested in Hanja characters and wanted to study them. Unfortunately, the online dictionary's Hanja dictionary is less than ideal, as its example words are not listed in order of usage or relevance.

To get a resource with a better order, I instead had a separate app installed. However, this app did not have a desktop or web version, so now I had different resource accessibility on different devices.

I later began using Anki, a flashcard program, to systematically reencounter the same words in the same context repeatedly. Making Anki flashcards was tedious, as I had to copy and paste information both the online dictionary and the Hanja app for almost every card. There are tools to make the process faster, but they fail to solve another problem I had: a flashcard's information only exists in Anki. If I ever looked at the online dictionary entry again for the word, none of my work would be there.

I was using the online dictionary and the Hanja app because I liked the built-in large number of words and information they provided. But with Anki, I liked the ability to make my own cards, with sentences and contexts I personally found and experienced.

Later, I looked inside of a paper dictionary and saw that the previous owner had left a number of notes for themselves in the margins of each page. They wrote example sentences and drew pictures right on the page, without the need to copy the definition from the dictionary to some other location. And whenever they looked that word up again, all of their writings would still be there.

Recreating this experience in a digital format is the major premise of this Korean dictionary. Rather than copy and pasting to Anki, you can add notes, pictures, and example sentences directly into the entry for any word. You can mark words as 'known' and the dictionary will specifically prioritize those words when ordering Hanja words. And most importantly, there are no separate flashcards. Here, the entire dictionary is now the set of flashcards, and choosing to study any word just involves adding the dictionary entry into your study list.

This is all also user specific, so every user has their own "copy" of the dictionary they can personally edit.

The dictionary also includes standard dictionary features like being able to click on any word to search for its meaning. For a complete list of features, view the repository for the dictionary.
